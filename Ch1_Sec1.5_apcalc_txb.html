Question 1: Find the limit by factoring.

\[ \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3} \]

Factor the numerator and simplify:

\[ \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{(x - 3)(x + 3)}{x - 3} = \lim_{x \to 3} (x + 3) \]

Substitute \(x = 3\):

\[ 3 + 3 = 6 \]

Question 4: Find the limit from the graph. Does the limit exist?

\[ \lim_{x \to 2} f(x) \]

Evaluate the one-sided limits:

\[ \lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x) = 3 \] \[ \lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x) = 1 \]

Since the left-hand limit and the right-hand limit are not equal, the overall limit does not exist (DNE).

Question 5: Find the limit by factoring.

\[ \lim_{x \to 5} \frac{x^2 - 25}{x - 5} \]

Factor the numerator using the difference of squares formula:

\[ \lim_{x \to 5} \frac{(x-5)(x+5)}{x-5} \]

Cancel out the common term \((x-5)\) and substitute \(x = 5\):

\[ \lim_{x \to 5} (x + 5) = 5 + 5 = 10 \]

\[ \lim_{x \to 5^+} \frac{x + 5}{x - 5} \]

\[ \lim_{x \to 5^+} \frac{\text{positive}}{\text{small positive}} = +\infty \]
